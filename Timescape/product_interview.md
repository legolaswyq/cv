Timescapes Interview Prep – Round 2 with Shehan (CPO)
About Timescapes (What We Know)
Founded: 2017, New Zealand
Product: Camera-based visual progress tracking for complex construction projects
Core value prop: Shared visibility → stay on schedule, validate claims, communicate progress
Markets: Australia, Canada, New Zealand, United States
Stage: Rapidly growing, small but scaling team
Culture signals: First principles thinking, speed + quality, deliberate iteration, "why"-obsessed
The Camera Product (/cameras)
Timescapes installs time-lapse/live cameras on construction sites. The ML angle is likely:

Object/activity detection (what's happening on site)
Progress tracking over time (how much has been built)
Anomaly detection (delays, safety issues)
Automated reporting / claim validation using visual evidence
Round 2 Purpose
"Provide context, understand career history, answer questions"

Shehan is the Chief Product Officer — he's evaluating whether you understand the product and business context, not just the ML tech. He wants to know:

Can you connect ML work to customer value?
Do you ask good "why" questions?
Are you a collaborative product-minded engineer?
Questions to Ask Shehan
🔍 Understanding the Product & ML Roadmap
"What's the single biggest unsolved problem that ML could address in your product today?" (Shows you think in terms of impact, not just capability)
"How do customers currently consume insights from the cameras — is it more passive monitoring or active querying? Where do you see that evolving?"
"Are there specific construction phases or site types (civil vs. commercial vs. institutional) where the CV models perform best and worst today?"
"How do you think about the balance between real-time inference on-site (edge) vs. cloud processing? Is that a live tension in the product?"
🧠 Team & Ways of Working
"As the second ML hire, how do you see the collaboration between ML and the product team working day-to-day? Does product define requirements, or is it more exploratory?"
"How does the team currently decide when to use an off-the-shelf model vs. investing in custom training? Is there a framework, or is it case-by-case?"
"What does a typical experiment-to-production cycle look like here? How long from idea to deployed feature?"
🏗️ Business & Strategy
"Construction is notoriously fragmented — different workflows across firms and regions. How does that affect how you design and train ML models?"
"With expansion into the US and Canada, are there ML challenges that are market-specific — different site types, regulations, or data availability?"
"What does success look like for this role in the first 6 months?" (Classic, but critical — helps you understand real priorities vs. the job description)
Things to Emphasise About Yourself
Given Shehan is CPO, lead with product thinking, not just technical depth:

Connect ML to outcomes: Don't just say "I built a CV model" — say "I built a CV model that reduced manual QA time by X, which let the team ship faster."
"Why" orientation: Share examples where you pushed back on a spec or asked upstream questions before building.
Iteration mindset: Show you're comfortable with v1 being imperfect — align with their "editing" value.
Customer empathy: If you've worked in domains with domain experts (non-engineers as end users), highlight it.
Quick Company Research Tips
Before Friday, check:

 Timescapes LinkedIn page – recent hires, team size, any press
 Their blog or case studies on timescapes.co
 Shehan's LinkedIn – his background, what he's posted about, prior companies
 Any news about construction tech / AI in construction (shows awareness of their market)
 App Store or reviews if they have a mobile component
Red Flags to Listen For
Unclear ownership between ML and product on priorities
No existing ML infrastructure (you'd be building from scratch with no support)
High churn of early employees
Vague answers about what "value" the first ML hire delivered
Good luck on Friday — the role sounds like a genuinely interesting intersection of CV, LLMs, and real-world construction workflows.
